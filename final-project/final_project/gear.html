Surfboard types

Boards come in different lengths and styles.
Tap each board for more details

GUN
The surfboard you want when the waves are huge. Typically ranges in length from 6'6" to 10', which allows the surfer to gain enough speed while paddling to catch the huge and fast moving waves. Designed for big drops and handling very high speeds with good control.

SUP
A large, wide, buoyant board from 10' to 12' in length, designed to allow the surfer to surf while standing up with the aid of a paddle.

LONGBOARD
Longboards typically range from 8 feet to 12 feet long. This extra volume allows them to paddle incredibly well and catch waves with ease. A beginner should start on a longboard shape to learn wave selection, paddling technique, and turning basics.

FUNBOARD
Not quite as long as a longboard, these typically range from 6 feet to 8 feet long. The funboard is a perfect for surfers who want to transition to a smaller board while still maintaining additional paddling power and stability. The funboard can come in a variety of tail shapes, nose shapes, and foils.

HYBRID
This type of board mixes the characteristics and performance of a shortboard with the stability and tail design of a fish. A great board for medium size waves.

SHORTBOARD
Shortboards are highly maneuverable and suitable for fast, steep waves in the 1' to 8' range. Their design reinvented high performance surfing in the 1970s and continues to allow surfers to push the boundaries of the sport. A favorite among professional surfers.

FISH
shorter and wider than most short boards, fish surfboards typically are equipped with two to three fins and are extremely agile in small- to medium-sized surf. Experienced surfers love them because they go fast in slower-breaking waves. A fun alternative to a shortboard.

In warm weather, you can surf in your bathing suit. As the water and air temps get colder, you'll want to wear either a wetsuit top, spring suit, or full wetsuit. In colder months, you will need gloves, booties and a hood. Wetsuits come in different thicknesses for extra warmth.


Wetsuits are made of a synthetic material called foam neoprene, which provides insulation to help your body combat heat loss in cold water. Unlike a dry suit, a wetsuit isn’t completely watertight, and allows small amounts of water to enter the suit, usually through the arm, leg and neck openings or through the zipper. This thin layer of water remains against your skin, but is much easier for your body to warm than the surrounding water outside the suit. Wetsuits are also designed to minimize the exchange of water between the interior and exterior, so your body doesn’t have to work as hard to stay warm. The thicker the neoprene, the more insulation it provides.
